About

Robotics education rooted in East Africa

StoryStem is a Nairobi-based robotics program. We teach electronics and programming through story-driven, hands-on experiences — following Amani, a 16-year-old student preparing for Nairobi Hack Weekend. Every session connects technical concepts to characters, problems, and places kids recognise.

  • 🔧
    Real engineering, not a toy

    Students wire Arduino boards, motor drivers, ultrasonic sensors, and Bluetooth modules from scratch.

  • 📖
    Story-driven curriculum

    Our 48-page builder's guide follows Amani's journey — African context, real problems, genuine engineering decisions.

  • 🏫
    Proven in Kenyan schools

    Delivered across multiple schools, a public bootcamp in Karen, and praised by parents and teachers at every session.

Programs

Three ways to bring StoryStem to your students

10 weekly sessions for Years 7–8. Groups of 5–10 students. Each student learns to build circuits, write code, and present their work.

Flagship

Full Program — Student Keeps Robot

Everything included: 10 facilitated sessions, complete kit, builder's guide, GitHub resources, and a StoryStem Builder Certificate. The student takes home the working robot they built.

School partnership

School Owns the Kits

School purchases kits once and reuses them each term. Includes 10 weekly sessions, guide per student, and certificates. Ideal for embedding robotics across year groups.

Entry point

Sessions Only

10 weekly sessions with a trained facilitator, builder's guide, and certificate. School provides or sources its own components. Great for trialling before committing to kits.
